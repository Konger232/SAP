ABOUT ME

My name is Ping. Born in Hong Kong, I spent my high school years in the United States, an experience shaped my creative perspective. Today, I'm based in Bangkok, Thailand, where I continue to explore design, technology, and culture across borders.

I am currently working toward a Digital Media Design graduate degree at Harvard Extension School. My academic focus reflects my long-standing interest in the intersection of design, user experience, and technology.

My background includes experience in web development and interface design, with hands-on work in user interface design and prototyping using HTML5, CSS, and JavaScript. I also have experience with Python and have been exposed to the Django framework, which has helped me understand how design and development connect on both the front end and back end.

I decided to take this course because I want to dive deeper into React.js and learn how to build modern, responsive, and interactive web applications. After spending time away from my role as a user interface designer and business analyst, this course is a perfect opportunity to refresh my development skills and explore how to create user interfaces that adapt smoothly across devices.

Outside of my academic and professional work, I stay creatively and socially engaged by volunteering with my local scout troop and helping with theatre staging at my children's school, where I enjoy supporting creative projects behind the scenes. I also love to travel and keep a sketchbook with me to capture observations, environments, and everyday moments from my surroundings.

One place that continues to inspire me is the beach, which represents calm, clarity, and balance in my life. It's where I feel most grounded and creatively refreshed.

When I'm in Bangkok, one of my favorite places to eat is Rongros, a restaurant located across from Wat Arun (the Sunset Temple) in downtown Bangkok, which I often recommend for both its atmosphere and its food.
